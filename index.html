The Problem

You have been inside this manuscript long enough to lose the thread of what it actually is.

The feedback has named things — pacing, stakes, voice — without locating the structure beneath them. You have revised toward those notes and come out the other side still uncertain. Not because the writing is wrong. Because the frame around it has been.

Most feedback evaluates your work against a story it was never trying to be.

A lyric novel is not a failed thriller. A hybrid memoir is not a broken narrative nonfiction. Every form has its own internal logic. When that logic is evaluated against a generic model, the diagnosis is always slightly wrong. The revision notes point to symptoms. The structure beneath them stays invisible.

NIM evaluates your manuscript against its declared form. What it finds is not what is wrong with your writing. It is what your writing is actually doing — and where that diverges from what the form requires.

The Method

A structured system that reads the manuscript, not the reputation.

The Narrative Intelligence Method identifies the active forces already present in your writing: how the narrative moves, how attachment is built, how meaning accumulates, what sustains attention over time. These forces are mapped against each other — not against an external checklist — to surface alignment, tension, and structural blind spots invisible from inside the work.

Direction The engine beneath the prose

What moves the story forward. The structural current that carries the reader from one moment to the next.

Connection The relational current

What binds the reader to character and voice. The bond that makes them care what happens next.

Meaning The resonant layer

What gives the story weight beyond its events. The thematic and symbolic architecture beneath the surface.

Desire The forward pull

What compels the reader to keep reading. The engine of narrative tension and sustained engagement.

The Hearthprint

Every evaluation produces a document built for your manuscript alone.

A structural map showing how the four forces interact in your specific work — where they hold, where they fracture, and where they diverge from the form you are writing toward.
