You have been inside this manuscript long enough to lose the thread of what it actually is.

The feedback has named things — pacing, stakes, voice — without locating the structure beneath them. You have revised toward those notes and come out the other side still uncertain. Not because the writing is wrong. Because the frame around it has been.

The problem is not your manuscript

Most feedback evaluates your work against a story it was never trying to be.

A lyric novel is not a failed thriller. A hybrid memoir is not a broken narrative nonfiction. Every form has its own internal logic — its own relationship between structure, voice, and meaning. When that logic is evaluated against a generic model, the diagnosis is always slightly wrong. The revision notes point to symptoms. The structure beneath them stays invisible.

NIM evaluates your manuscript against its declared form. What it finds is not what is wrong with your writing. It is what your writing is actually doing — and where that diverges from what the form requires.

The Method

A structured system that reads the manuscript, not the reputation.

The Narrative Intelligence Method identifies the active forces already present in your writing: how the narrative moves, how attachment is built, how meaning accumulates, what sustains attention over time. These forces are mapped against each other — not against an external checklist — to surface alignment, tension, and structural blind spots that are invisible from inside the work.

What emerges is a coherent model of what your manuscript is doing. Not what someone thinks of it. What it is.

The Four Forces

Direction
What moves the story forward. The engine beneath the prose.
Connection
What binds the reader to character and voice. The relational current.
Meaning
What gives the story weight beyond its events. The resonant layer.
Desire
What compels the reader to keep reading. The forward pull.
