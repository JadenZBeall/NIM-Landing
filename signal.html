Arriving from NIM Signal Scan

The Signal Scan identifies where intent and execution separate. It does not determine whether the manuscript can be made to work.

If your report identified structural inconsistency, that result is not unusual. It reflects a limitation of surface-level analysis — not a verdict on the manuscript.

The question is not whether inconsistency exists.

The question is whether the structure can support the intended outcome.

Surface-level analysis locates the gap. It cannot map what lies beneath it — the structural mechanics that determine whether a manuscript actually delivers what it intends.

That requires full structural diagnosis.

The Hearthprint Evaluation maps what the manuscript actually does, identifies failure points at the system level, and determines whether coherence can be restored.
